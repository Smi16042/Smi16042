Visitor Analysis and Persona

Forbes.com

http://www.forbes.com/

Target Audience

Section 1. Demograpics

  • Age; the average age of the user is 20 to 40 years of age.
  • Income; the average income range is 70,000-100,000 US dollars.
  • Education; the average education is a masters in College
  • Geographic Location; 75% of viewers are in the United States, 25% are from other countries.

Section 2. Needs of the Visitors

1. Forbes provides knowledge about Business, Technical, Social, and other important aspects business men and women may find interesting.

2. Forbes provides analysis on the current economical state of the world.

3. Forbes provides orginazation and structural design on the website that allows you to easily find specific documents.

4. Forbes talks about current issues that the consumer may want to know about.

Section 3. Content Organization

Forbes orgainzed there content in a few ways. at the very top they had a sidebar with editorial and staff picks, they also had a table that would pop up if you hovered over their Forbes logo at the top left which would show a list of topics that you could pick through. After that they allowed you to scroll down and showed popular articles and graphs, each section devided by a title and definition like "most popular" or "markets".

Section 4. Mobile Site Enjoyability

Several things that made this experience survivable was that it had some features that were extremely usefull for the smaller device like,

Text Wrap

Good Zoom functions

Easy scrolling

Not assualted by giant adds / popups

Very Smooth loading and movement

Section 5. Faciliates Mobile Enviroment?

Yes I believe that Forbes did create the website with the idea of a mobile user in mind, it provides the information in a organized and effective way on the mobile, which the target audience would enjoy.

It does this by having tables, sidebars, and images that the user can touch to get to the data that they want to see.
